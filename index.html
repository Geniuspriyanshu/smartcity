Deep Dive — Integrated Mobility Hub (Example)

Overview

This case study outlines the planning, stakeholders, tech stack, deployment, KPIs, lessons learned and next phases of an integrated mobility hub that connects buses, trams, bikes, and micro-transit with real-time coordination.

Phases

  • Phase 0: Stakeholder alignment & feasibility (6 months)
  • Phase 1: Pilot microtransit + fleet telemetry (12 months)
  • Phase 2: City-wide adaptive signals & demand prediction (18 months)
  • Phase 3: Full multimodal integration and fare consolidation (24 months)

Tech Stack

Edge devices (LoRaWAN / NB-IoT), MQTT brokers, time-series DB, stream processors, analytics, cloud-hosted APIs, and mobile apps for citizens and operators.

KPIs

  1. Average trip time reduction target: 15%
  2. Modal shift to public transport: 10% increase
  3. Operational cost reduction: 8%

Lessons & Risks

Early stakeholder engagement, data privacy design, maintenance plan for sensors, and a clear business model are the most important success factors.
